Figure | Audited candidate-level BTLA multiomics evidence.

The matrix summarises candidate regulator measurements in the BTLA+TCR versus TCR contrast across transcriptomics, whole proteomics and phosphoproteomics. Candidate mRNA is shown as transcript-derived contextual evidence because the ranked regulators were nominated as upstream explanations of the BTLA transcriptional response rather than selected for changes in their own expression. Protein abundance and phosphosite changes are classified as independent observed molecular associations. Primary calls used the source-defined thresholds: adjusted p<0.05 and |log2 fold change|>log2(1.5) for transcriptomics, and adjusted p<0.05 and |log2 fold change|>log2(1.3) for protein and phosphosite measurements. Rows are grouped by model-specific or shared nomination. Grey cells indicate measured candidates without a qualifying signal; blank or separately marked cells indicate candidates not measured or outside the assay universe. Molecular direction was not interpreted as supportive or opposing because both model rankings were unsigned.

Coverage and qualifying-association summary. Of 43 candidates, 41 were measured for mRNA (BTLA-specific qualifying = 0), 16 for protein (qualifying = 2: SMAP2 at 4h, STAT5B at 20min), and 5 for phosphosites (qualifying = 5: MYC, GTF3C2, HIRIP3, STAU2, TFDP1). In total 7 of 43 candidates showed an independent molecular association (protein or phosphosite): MYC, SMAP2, STAT5B, GTF3C2, HIRIP3, STAU2, TFDP1; none were shared candidates (GREmLN-specific 4, shared 0, GENIE3-specific 3). No candidate showed a BTLA-specific qualifying mRNA change; the ~10-15 mRNA signals in the earlier 08e chart arose from the TCR-vs-unstimulated contrast and a seed-inclusive candidate set (see mrna_discrepancy_report.md). Under the stricter harmonised threshold (|log2FC|>=0.585) the seven independent associations are unchanged.
